Rick and Morty is an American television series of adult animation created by Justin Roiland and Dan Harmon to Adult Swim. The series follows the misadventures of a scientist, Rick, and his easily influenced grandson, Morty, who spend time between the domestic life and space, temporary and intergalactic travel. Roiland is in charge of giving voice to the two main characters, the series also includes the voices of Chris Parnell, Spencer Grammer, and Sarah Chalke.

The origins of the series can be found in a short film rudimentary animated by Roiland for Channel 101 film festival called Doc and Mharti, a parody of the main protagonists of Back to the Future. NBC dismissed Harmon for arguments between him and the Community series executives, and then he and Roiland developed a series based on the recently released movie. The series has been universally acclaimed for its originality, creativity and humor.
